Settling In: My Freshers' Week Experience

My Experiences of Moving In and Freshers' Week

By Frank Dong | December 3, 2024, 13:35

Moving into university was a whirlwind of emotions. Excitement, nerves, and curiosity all swirled together as I arrived at my new home for the year. With four suitcases and a backpack in tow, I stepped into my student accommodation, Astor College. The buzz of other students unpacking and introducing themselves was both intimidating and thrilling.

After setting up my room, which I made cozy with decorations from home, I ventured out to meet my flatmates. It was a relief to find everyone just as eager to make friends. Conversations quickly turned to where everyone was from and what they were studying - a mix of accents and cultures filled the air, reminding me of how diverse UCL is.

Freshers' Week was everything I expected and more. From welcome talks to late-night events, every day was packed with opportunities to dive headfirst into university life.

The highlight for me was joining societies. I signed up for snowsports, tennis, the film society, and a couple of finance and entrepreneurship groups. Each society had something unique to offer, and their enthusiasm made it hard to say no. I even found myself chatting about skiing trips and tennis meetups with people I'd just met, which felt surreal but exciting.

One of the most memorable moments was the Freshers' Fair. Walking through the stalls, I felt like a kid in a candy store, except this time, the treats were new experiences waiting to be had. Between grabbing freebies and chatting with society members, I realized how much UCL had to offer beyond academics.

It wasn't all smooth sailing, though. Balancing social events with settling in was tricky. There were moments when I felt overwhelmed by the constant activity and the pressure to make the most of every moment.

I also underestimated how tiring it would be to meet so many new people. Names and faces blurred together, and I quickly learned to embrace asking, “Sorry, what was your name again?” without embarrassment.

Freshers' Week taught me a lot about stepping out of my comfort zone. Whether it was joining a group for karaoke night (despite my terrible singing) or striking up a conversation with someone in the elevator, these little moments built my confidence.

I also learned the importance of taking breaks. By the middle of the week, I set aside time to recharge - a quiet evening in my room with a cup of tea and a movie worked wonders.

Now that Freshers' Week has come to an end, I feel ready to dive into the rest of the university experience. I'm excited to explore London, get stuck into my course, and deepen the connections I've started to make.

Moving in and Freshers' Week have been a rollercoaster of new beginnings, but if these first few days are anything to go by, I know the rest of the year will be unforgettable.
